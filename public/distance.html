Understanding Distance vs. Displacement

Distance is the total path an object travels, while displacement is how far it ends up from where it started in a straight line.

  • Distance is always positive and counts every twist and turn
  • Displacement only depends on the starting and ending positions (and can have direction)

Instructions:

Click where you would like to set your starting point.

Click additional points to create a path.

Press Play to watch the object move along the path.

Watch how Distance increases with every step, while Displacement only depends on where you are relative to the start.

Try making loops or returning to the start — notice Distance stays large but Displacement becomes small or zero.
